Mushroom Bisque

Description

Mushroom Bisque is a velvety, indulgent soup that celebrates the earthy flavors of mushrooms. Sauteed with onions and garlic, the mushrooms create a rich and robust basem while a touch of thyme addes depth. Blended to a creamy texture and infused with either cream or a non-dairy alternative, this comforting bisque deights the palate with its smoothness. A drizzle of truffle oil or a sprinkle of chives elevates its presentation, making it a perfect choice for a luxurious appetizer or a comforting meal on its own.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) fresh mushrooms (white, cremini, or a mix), sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups (1 liter) vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream or half-and-half
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: fresh chopped parsley, a drizzle of truffle oil, or a dollop of sour cream.

Steps

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saute until they become translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, being careful not to let it brown.
  3. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the sliced mushrooms to the pot. Cook the mushrooms until they release their moisture and start to brown, stirring occasionally. This should take about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Once the mushrooms have browned, season with dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine the flavors.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium. Push the mushrooms and onions to one side of the pot, creating an empty space. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter to the empty space and let it melt. Once melted, sprinkle the flour over the butter and whisk it together to form a roux. Cook the roux for about a minute to remove the raw flour taste.
  6. Gradually pour the vegetable or chicken broth into the pot while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Bring the soup to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  7. Using an immersion blender or transferring the soup in batches to a blender, puree the soup until smooth. If using a regular blender, be cautious with hot liquids, and blend in small batches to avoid splatters.
  8. Return the pureed soup to the pot and stir in the heavy cream or half-and-half. Adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Let the soup simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  9. Serve hot, garnishing each bowl with optional toppings like fresh chopped parsley, a drizzle of truffle oil, or a dollop of sour cream.
  10. Enjoy your delicious and comforting mushroom soup!
